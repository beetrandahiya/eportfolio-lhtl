Learning Through Inquiry


I discovered what inquiry-based learning entails.
It's a method of instruction that encourages pupils to engage in problem-solving and hands-on learning.
It consists of five steps: "asking, investigating, creating, discussing, and reflecting" (conclusion).
"Curiosity" is also the driving force behind discovery, inquiry, and learning. Inquiry-based learning, in my opinion, is a really successful way. It is a problem-based learning approach that incorporates critical thinking abilities. "Tell me and I forget," "Show me or Teach me and I will remember," and "Involve me and I will learn." Inquiry-based learning is essentially asking questions and seeking knowledge, and it develops the habit of inquiring. It aids in the retention of knowledge. When we actively pursue details, explore information, or investigate a subject, we recall more information, which is exactly what inquiry-based learning is supposed to achieve.
